### **Pricing Localization**

**Principles**:
1. **Price in local currency** (eliminate FX risk for customers)
2. **Adjust for purchasing power** (India gets 50% discount, not "price in rupees")
3. **Simplify tax compliance** (VAT in EU, GST in India, etc.)

**Pricing Adjustments** (vs US baseline)
| Region | Adjustment | Rationale |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| Canada, UK, Australia | Par | Similar GDP/capita, mature markets |
| Western Europe | Par | High GDP/capita, strong purchasing power |
| Eastern Europe | -20% | Lower wages, price-sensitive |
| Singapore, Japan, Korea | +10% | Premium markets, quality expectations |
| India | -50% | Affordability, high-volume play |
| LATAM | -30% | Lower purchasing power, currency volatility |
| Middle East | Par | High purchasing power (oil-rich) |
| Africa | -40% | Emerging markets, build future base |

---

### **Compliance & Data Residency**

**Region-Specific Requirements**

**Europe (GDPR)**
- Data Processing Agreements (DPA) with every customer
- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- Data portability
- Privacy Impact Assessments (PIA)
- Appoint EU representative (required if no EU office)

**Germany (Extra Strict)**
- Schrems II compliance (no data transfer to US without explicit consent)
- German data centers (Frankfurt, Munich)
- German-language privacy policy

**China**
- Cybersecurity Law requires local data storage
- Government approval for cross-border data transfer
- Likely need Chinese partner (JV model)
- **Decision**: Delay China entry until 2029+ (complexity)

**India**
- Reserve Bank of India (RBI) mandates local storage for payment data
- Data Protection Bill (pending, similar to GDPR)
- Mumbai data center required for fintech customers

**Brazil**
- LGPD (Brazilian GDPR)
- Local Data Protection Authority (ANPD)
- Data localization for sensitive sectors (finance, healthcare)
