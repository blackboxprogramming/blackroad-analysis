## Quick Reference: What Goes Where

1. **STAT** - Shocking number + pain point
2. **WHY NOW** - "just became" + transformation
3. **WHY CHANGE** - Time/energy waste equation
4. **WHY CARE** - "feels like" emotional payoff
5. **WHY CHANGE WHAT YOU'RE COMFORTABLE** - Acknowledge what works BUT...
6. **WHY CONSIDER** - "What if" question
7. **LANDSCAPE** - Industry dichotomy (expensive vs painful)
8. **WHAT YOU MIGHT USUALLY SEE** - List 4 bad industry standards
9. **WHAT WE OFFER** - Your simple solution in one line
10. **SOCIAL PROOF** - Customer quote with numbers
11. **WHAT WE FIX** - The gap/transformation
12. **WHAT WE CONSIDER** - List 6 things you handle automatically
13. **WHAT WE MAKE EASIER** - Activity transformation
14. **WHAT WE TAKE OFF YOUR HANDS** - List 4 responsibilities removed
15. **WHAT WE WOULDN'T WANT YOU WORRYING ABOUT** - 2 specific fears
16. **WHAT IS NECESSARY** - Acknowledge need, reject old pain
17. **WHAT IS FREEDOM** - 3-part benefit list
18. **WHAT IS A DECISION** - Simple binary choice
19. **DRIVER'S SEAT** - Empowerment description with analogy
20. **WHAT HAVE WE SEEN** - Pattern recognition (numbers + insight)
21. **OBJECTION** - "You might think: [quote]"
22. **OBJECTION RESPONSE** - Proof/reversal
23. **FUTURE PACING** - "Imagine" + 4 specific scenarios
24. **COST COMPARISON** - Old way cost vs your price with comparison
25. **CTA** - One word + arrow

## Writing Rules

### Language Patterns
- **"just became"** - creates urgency (WHY NOW)
- **"feels like"** - emotional transformation (WHY CARE)
- **"What if"** - opens imagination (WHY CONSIDER)
- **"You know X works. But..."** - acknowledge then pivot
- **"Most platforms offer: A. B. C. D."** - list bad options
- **"We offer: X. Y. Z."** - list simple solution
- **Direct customer quote** - must include numbers or specific transformation
- **"We wouldn't want you ever worrying about X or Y"** - specific fears
- **"X is necessary. Y, Z, and W aren't."** - separate need from pain
- **"The decision is simple: X or Y"** - binary choice
- **"Being in the driver's seat means..."** - empowerment
- **"like [ANALOGY]"** - make abstract concrete (electricity, breathing, etc.)
- **"You might think: [objection]"** - quote their internal voice
- **"Imagine [scenario]. [scenario]. [scenario]."** - future pace with list
- **"X costs $Y. Compare that to Z."** - price anchor

### Numbers to Include
- Stat percentage (87%)
- Time waste (15+ hours weekly)
- Gap duration (6-month gap)
- Social proof numbers (10,000 lines deleted, 3 features)
- Scale proof (10,000+ applications)
- Price comparison (one hour at $150 vs $49/month)

### Lists to Build
- **3-part acknowledgment**: "You know A works. B scales. C provisions."
- **3-part cost**: "But they cost you X, Y, and Z."
- **4-part bad options**: Industry standard failures
- **3-part simple offer**: Your solution broken down
- **6-part automation**: What you handle automatically
- **4-part responsibility removal**: What they never touch again
- **2-part fears**: Specific worries you eliminate
- **3-part freedom**: What good looks like
- **3-part empowerment**: What driver's seat means
- **4-part future pacing**: Imagine scenarios
